# 700 Calorie Roasted Chicken Brown Rice Dinner

A 3-serving dinner built around a 2 lb pack of skinless chicken breasts, brown rice, and a large vegetable side.

## Dinner serving

Each dinner is:

- 1 roasted chicken breast
- 60 g dry brown rice, cooked, or about 160-180 g cooked brown rice
- 250 g broccoli or green beans
- 150 g baby carrots

Approximate dinner total: 670-730 calories, depending mostly on chicken breast size.

## Batch ingredients

Makes 3 servings.

### Roasted chicken

- 1 2 lb pack skinless chicken breasts, usually 3 breasts
- Iodized salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- Garlic powder, to taste
- Smoked paprika, to taste
- Optional: Italian seasoning, to taste
- Optional: spray avocado oil, light spray only

### Rice

- 180 g dry brown rice
- Water according to rice cooker directions

### Vegetables and sides

- 750 g broccoli or green beans
- 450 g baby carrots

## Instructions

1. Thaw the chicken in the refrigerator for 36-48 hours if frozen.
2. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
3. Season the chicken breasts with iodized salt, black pepper, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and Italian seasoning if using.
4. Optionally use a light spray of avocado oil to help the seasoning stick.
5. Roast the chicken until the thickest part reaches 165°F on an instant-read thermometer.
6. Rest the chicken for 5-10 minutes before portioning.
7. Cook the brown rice in the rice cooker.
8. Steam or microwave the broccoli or green beans.
9. Portion each dinner with 1 chicken breast, 1/3 of the cooked rice, 250 g vegetables, and 150 g baby carrots.

## Storage

- Store the chicken, rice, and cooked vegetables in meal-prep containers.
- Keep baby carrots separate if preferred for crunch.
- Refrigerate portions planned within 4 days.
- Reheat chicken, rice, and vegetables in the microwave until hot.

## Notes

- This recipe assumes one whole breast per serving rather than weighing cooked chicken.
- Because the chicken breasts vary in size, this is naturally a range rather than an exact 700 calories.
- Do not add avocado by default; one large chicken breast plus rice already gets close to the target.
- If a chicken breast is unusually small, add a piece of fresh fruit or an extra slice of toast.
- If a chicken breast is unusually large, keep the rice at 60 g dry per serving and do not add extra sides.
